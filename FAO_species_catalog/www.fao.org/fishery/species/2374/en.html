        Dentex canariensis (Steindachner, 1881)

        Synonyms
      •  
        		• Dentex cuninghami  non Regan, 1905. Norman, 1935: 15.
      •  
        		• Dentex nufar  non Valenciennes, 1830. Poll, 1971: 13
        FAO Names
        En - Canary dentex, Fr - Denté à tache rouge, Sp - Chacarona de Canarias.
        3Alpha Code: DEN     Taxonomic Code: 1703906004
        Scientific Name with Original Description
        Dentex canariensis  Steindachner, 1881. Sitzber. Akad. Wiss. Wien. 83: 393 (Canary Islands). Type: presumably in NMW (Naturhistorisches Museum, Wien).
        Diagnostic Features
        Body oval, rather deep and compressed. Head profile regulary convex except for a slight hump on front; eye diameter smaller than width of suborbital space in large individuals.
          Cheeks scaly (7 to 9 rows); some small, scarcely visible scales also present on preopercle throughout its height.
          Mouth low, slightly oblique. Jaws subequal.
          Several rows of canine-like teeth, outer row the strongest with 4 to 6 very well developed anterior teeth in each jaw.
          Gillrakers on first arch 10 to 13 lower and 6 to 9 upper.
          Dorsal fin with 12 spines and 9 or 10 soft rays; first two spines very short, the following more or less filamentous and decreasing in length from the third or fourth backward. Anal fin with 3 spines and 8 or 9 soft rays of pelvic fins filamentous.
          Scales along lateral line 61 to 68.
          Colour reddish with silvery reflections. Belly ligther and head darker. A dark red spot posteriorly on base of dorsal fin extending well beyond scaly sheath. A dark area at pectoral fin axils; more or less aligned dark spots on soft portion of dorsal fin. Caudal fin dark red, very finely edged with black. In some individuals, a greenish yellow band between eyes. 
        Geographical Distribution
        Along the West African coast from Morocco (Cape Bojador) to Angola; absent around islands, including the Canaries. Recently recorded from coast of Cadiz, southern Spain (Otero, et al., 1998).

        Habitat and Biology
        Benthopelagic and demersal behaviour.Lives in small groups though large individuals may be solitary. Inhabiting various types of bottom, but especially rocky substrate usually to depths of about 150 m (rarely reported to 450 m), the depth range increasing with age.

        Sexual maturity is reached in the second year (about 20 cm) in the Northern part of the Gulf of Guinea, intermittent spawning occurs from July to September with a second shorter spawning period in January.
          Carnivorous, the young plankton-feeders, the adults feeding mainly on fish, crustaceans and cephalopods.
        Size
        Maximum 100 cm; common to 35 cm
        Interest to Fisheries
        A seasonal fishery linked to upwellings. Separate statistics are not reported for this species. Caught with bottom trawls, trammel nets and on line gear.
        Marketed fresh or frozen (flesh highly esteemed); also used for fishmeal and oil.

        Remarks
        CheimeriusSmith, 1938 is a valid subgenus. Trans. R. Soc. S. Afr., 26: 292 (type: Cheimerius nufar Valenciennes, 1830, by monotypy). Dentex nufar Valenciennes, 1830 is a valid species from the Indian Ocean.
